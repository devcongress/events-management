# Product

## Register

product

## Users

- DevCongress organizers working inside the console to prepare events, review submissions, collect feedback, and make quick operational decisions.
- Attendees submitting post-event feedback from a public form, usually on mobile or laptop shortly after a meetup ends.
- Testers and maintainers reviewing route-level feedback and event response signal to understand what landed and what needs follow-up.

Organizers usually arrive here in a task-focused mindset: they are checking status, configuring one specific event, or reading responses fast enough to act on them.

## Product Purpose

DevCon-Comm is the working console for running DevCongress community events end to end: event setup, talk review, attendance, event feedback, and lightweight reporting. Success means organizers can move from “what happened?” to “what should we do next?” without hunting across multiple screens or re-reading noisy UI.

## Brand Personality

Calm, capable, direct.

The product should feel like a thoughtful operations tool: confident without swagger, warm without fluff, and lightweight enough that the interface gets out of the way of the work.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboard tropes with oversized KPI tiles, decorative gradients, and fake “insight” language.
- Cluttered admin tools that force organizers to scan dense tables before they understand the next step.
- Fancy overlay-heavy UI that hides the core record or makes response review feel theatrical instead of useful.
- Marketing-style surfaces inside the organizer console.

## Design Principles

1. Put the next organizer action in plain sight.
2. Let structure carry the complexity, not extra copy.
3. Keep review surfaces skimmable first, detailed second.
4. Use familiarity as trust, especially for admin workflows.
5. Make public-facing forms easy to answer and organizer-facing results easy to act on.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Aim for WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-accessible interaction patterns across public and organizer surfaces.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences and avoid motion that delays task completion.
- Keep copy straightforward for mixed technical and non-technical attendees.
- Support fast scanning with clear hierarchy, consistent controls, and roomy hit targets on mobile.
